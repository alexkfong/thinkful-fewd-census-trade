Considering trade

During his first term as president of the United States,
Barack Obama pledged a "pivot" to the Pacific rim after years of foreign policy focused on the Middle East. The Trans-Pacific Partnership is a trade deal between 12 countries that purports to do just that by binding the economies of 11 Pacific trading partners closer to the U.S. But what's missing in this "pivot" is one glaring — and giant — omission: China. Here's a look at how trade between the U.S. and China compares with the countries to be included in the Trans-Pacific Partnership according to data from the U.S. Census Bureau.
